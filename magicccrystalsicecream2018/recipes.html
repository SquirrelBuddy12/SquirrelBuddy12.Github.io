Little known facts on Ice Cream!

  1. Some weird flavors of ice cream include Buckwheat ice cream, Beer flavored ice cream, and Parmesan Gelato.
  2. Most of the vanilla used to make ice cream comes from Madagascar & Indonesia.
  3. Top 5 countries consuming ice cream: US, New Zealand, Australia and Finland.
  4. Chocolate ice cream was invented before Vanilla.
  5. There is an ice cream fruit in Hawaii.There’s a Hawaiian fruit that tastes exactly like Vanilla ice cream. It’s called the Inga Feuillei, but locals call it the Ice Cream Bean.
  6. Chocolate syrup is the world's most popular ice cream topping.
  7. In 1904, an ice cream vendor ran out of cones. He was at the World’s Fair in St. Louis, Missouri, and he was facing high demand from guests. Desperate for a solution, he turned to a waffle vendor. Together, they came up with the idea to mold the waffles into cones and serve the ice cream in there.
  8. July is National Ice Cream Month.

Vanilla Ice Cream

Ingredients
4 cups organic milk 1 vanilla bean, split
3 tablespoons plus teaspoons cornstarch 1 cup sugar
1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1 teaspoon almond extract
Instructions
  1. Note: This is the gelato base for the Liquid Nitrogen Ice Cream.
  2. Place three cups of milk in a saucepan with the vanilla bean over medium-low heat.
  3. Add the sugar and the cornstarch, mix well.
  4. When the milk starts to simmer, remove it from the heat and pour in the cornstarch mixture, stirring the whole time.
  5. Return to the saucepan to medium-low heat and stir until it thickens up, 1-12 minutes. It should have the consistency of a milkshake.
  6. Pour the mixture through a strainer into a mixing bowl, whisk in the vanilla and almond extract, and let it cool on the counter for 20 minutes or so.
  7. Chill in the refrigerator for a few hours or overnight.
  8. You can use your Liquid Nitrogen or a standard ice cream maker to finish it.

Rock Sugar Crystals

Ingredients
1 cup water 3 cups table sugar
clean glass jar pencil or butter knife
string or yarn (do not use nylon string) pan for boiling water and making solution
spoon for stirring (optional) food coloring
Instructions
  1. Tie the string to your pencil or butter knife. The string should be able to hang into your jar without touching the bottom or sides.
  2. In the pan, start boiling the water.
  3. Stir in the sugar one spoonful at a time. You want to make sure there is lots of the solute (sugar) in the solution, but not so much that it doesn't all dissolve into the solution. If you have some undissolved sugar, your Rock Crystals will start building onto those molecules rather than your string.
  4. Add a few drops of food coloring if you want some color in your candy.
  5. Carefully pour the solution from the pan into your glass jar. Make sure it's clean, otherwise crystals will build onto those molecules on the jar and not on your string.
  6. Balance the pencil or butter knife (with the string tied to it) on the lip of the jar and let the string dangle into the solution.
  7. Find a safe spot for your jar so it won't get knocked around or exposed to too much dust and debris.
  8. Check on your Rock Crystals the next day and notice how they've started to take on the form of the string.
  9. Whenever your Rock Crystals are the right size for your taste or when they stop growing, go ahead and take them out of the jar and let them dry.
